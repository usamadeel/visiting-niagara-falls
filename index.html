Top Niagara Falls Attractions

From thundering boat rides to breathtaking observation towers — these are the experiences you can't miss when visiting Niagara Falls.

🚤

Maid of the Mist

The most iconic Niagara Falls attraction since 1846. Sail directly into the mist at the base of the American Falls and Horseshoe Falls aboard these legendary blue-poncho boats.

Niagara City Cruises

The Canadian equivalent of Maid of the Mist, offering stunning up-close views of the mighty Horseshoe Falls from the Ontario side. Red ponchos provided!

Cave of the Winds

Walk along the wooden Hurricane Deck just feet from the base of Bridal Veil Falls. An extraordinary experience that puts you inside the thunder of the falls. Sandals and ponchos provided.

📍 USA Side ⏱️ 45 Min 🗓️ May–Oct

Skylon Tower

Rise 775 feet above Niagara Falls for 360-degree views stretching across New York State, Ontario, and as far as Toronto on clear days. Features North America's only revolving dining room with falls views.

📍 Canada Side ⏱️ 1–2 Hours 🗓️ Year-Round
⭐ 4.7 Book Tickets

White Water Walk

Descend into the Niagara Gorge via tunnel and boardwalk to walk alongside Class VI white water rapids — some of the wildest in the world. An electrifying natural wonder just downstream from the falls.

📍 Canada Side ⏱️ 45 Min 🗓️ Apr–Oct
⭐ 4.8 Book Tickets

Descend 150 feet by elevator through solid rock tunnels to emerge behind the thundering curtain of Horseshoe Falls. A uniquely Canadian perspective on one of the world's greatest wonders.

Do I need a passport to visit both sides of Niagara Falls?

+

Yes, crossing between the US and Canada requires a valid passport (or NEXUS card for eligible travelers). For our full-day dual-side tours, we assist with border crossings and advise on all documentation requirements. Non-US/Canadian citizens should verify visa requirements before booking. Our guides handle all the logistics — you just show up with your valid travel documents.

What is the best time of year to visit Niagara Falls?

+

Niagara Falls is spectacular year-round! Summer (June–August) offers all attractions fully operational and warm weather — peak season. Fall (September–October) brings stunning foliage with smaller crowds and great temperatures. Winter offers the magical Winter Festival of Lights and icy, dramatic scenery. Spring (April–May) sees the falls at maximum flow from snowmelt. Our tours run 365 days a year!

What is included in the Full-Day Both Sides Tour?

+

Our Full-Day Tour includes: hotel pickup and drop-off, certified bilingual guide, Maid of the Mist or Niagara City Cruise boat ride, Cave of the Winds admission, Journey Behind the Falls access, Niagara Falls State Park entry, Skylon Tower observation deck, a welcome lunch, and all US and Canadian border crossing assistance. All admission tickets are included — no surprise charges!

How far in advance should I book my tour?

+

We recommend booking 1–2 weeks in advance, especially for summer peak season (June–August) and major holidays. Certain premium tours like the Illumination Dinner Tour can sell out weeks ahead. Last-minute bookings are sometimes available (check our website), but early booking guarantees your spot and often the best price. You can also cancel free up to 24 hours before.

Are your tours wheelchair/accessibility friendly?

+

Absolutely! We offer fully accessible tours with ADA/AODA-compliant vehicles, accessible viewing platforms at all major viewpoints, and wheelchair-friendly itineraries. Maid of the Mist, Niagara City Cruises, and Skylon Tower are all accessible. Cave of the Winds has limited accessibility. Please contact us when booking to ensure we arrange the best possible experience for your needs.

What is the difference between the American and Canadian sides?

+

The American side (Niagara Falls, NY) features America's oldest state park, the American Falls, Bridal Veil Falls, Cave of the Winds, and Maid of the Mist — great for getting close to the falls. The Canadian side (Niagara Falls, ON) offers the best panoramic views of all three waterfalls especially Horseshoe Falls, Niagara City Cruises, Journey Behind the Falls, Skylon Tower, and vibrant Clifton Hill entertainment. Most visitors agree both sides offer distinct experiences — which is why our Full-Day Both Sides Tour is our bestseller!
